My reordering strategy places the `visited` predicate first (as it is the goal predicate), followed by `at-robot` (action-enabling), then `connected` (static), and within the move action, I order preconditions with the static `connected` last and effects with the goal-achieving `visited` first, followed by the positive `at-robot` effect before the negative delete effect, to optimize H_Add_Rp_Fwd tie-breaking and novelty discrimination. Since this domain has only one action, the key leverage points are predicate ordering and precondition/effect ordering within that action to ensure goal-relevant fluents get the lowest internal IDs.

(define (domain grid-visit-all)
(:requirements :typing)
(:types        place - object)
(:predicates (visited ?x - place)
	     (at-robot ?x - place)
	     (connected ?x ?y - place)
)
	
(:action move
:parameters (?curpos ?nextpos - place)
:precondition (and (at-robot ?curpos) (connected ?curpos ?nextpos))
:effect (and (visited ?nextpos) (at-robot ?nextpos) (not (at-robot ?curpos)))
)

)
