I reordered the predicates to place the goal predicate `visited` first, following Madagascar’s backward-chaining heuristic that prioritizes goal variables with lower SAT IDs. I also swapped the action parameters to put the destination (the goal‑relevant variable) first, so that ground actions achieving `visited` receive lower variable IDs and are explored earlier by the solver.

```lisp
(define (domain grid-visit-all)
(:requirements :typing)
(:types        place - object)
(:predicates (visited ?x - place)
             (at-robot ?x - place)
             (connected ?x ?y - place)
)
(:action move
:parameters (?dest ?src - place)
:precondition (and (connected ?src ?dest) (at-robot ?src))
:effect (and (visited ?dest) (at-robot ?dest) (not (at-robot ?src)))
)
)
```
